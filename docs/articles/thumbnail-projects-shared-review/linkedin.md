Thumbnail Projects and Shared Review

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait generates thumbnails and saves them to your gallery. Review Gallery adds projects, shareable links, and comments so creators and small teams can pick a winner before anything goes live.

**The problem**
People screenshot options and send them around. Comments land in five apps. Versions get mixed up. The file that goes live is not always the one the team agreed on. Gallery view solved personal history, but not campaign memory or second opinions.

**The solution**
We built **Review Gallery**. Group thumbnails into projects, share a view-only link, and collect comments on the cards before anything publishes.

**How it works**
Create a project for a video or campaign and assign your best variations. Copy a share link. Viewers open a clean gallery at a public URL without needing the full studio or an account. Share all thumbnails or favorites only for a tight shortlist. Signed-in owners and editors comment directly on each option. Editors can join through a separate flow when you want shared settings, not open mutation on view links.

**The result**
Feedback stays attached to the image under debate. One link, one shortlist, comments on the cards. Teams pick a winner before YouTube sees anything.

**Bottom line**
When your product outputs visual options, give users a view-only share path and put comments on the asset. Review is a feature, not a screenshot workflow. Try it at viewbait.app.
