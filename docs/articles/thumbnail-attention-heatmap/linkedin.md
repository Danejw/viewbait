Thumbnail Attention Heatmap

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait generates YouTube thumbnails and connects to your channel on Pro. Attention Lens overlays a predicted heatmap on any thumbnail so you can sanity-check focal hierarchy before you publish.

**The problem**
Creators judge thumbnails on a big screen. Viewers see them tiny and fast. Text, faces, and backgrounds fight each other, and nobody notices until after upload. Gut feel at desktop size is a weak pre-flight check.

**The solution**
We built **Attention Lens**, a one-click heatmap on any thumbnail card. It shows where viewers are likely to look first so you can fix layout before the video goes live.

**How it works**
Tap the heatmap icon on a generated thumbnail or a YouTube upload. ViewBait sends the exact image on screen to the model and returns an overlay you can toggle on and off. Results cache on the card so comparing finalists does not cost another call. Advanced and Pro only, hidden in the UI and enforced in the API.

**The result**
Creators catch weak layouts during shortlisting, not from post-mortem analytics. Usage clusters on final candidates, which is exactly when a sanity check matters.

**Bottom line**
Give users a pre-flight check on the asset they are already staring at. Judgment features belong on the decision surface, not in a separate report. See it at viewbait.app.
