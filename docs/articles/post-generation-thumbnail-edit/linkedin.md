Post-Generation Thumbnail Edit

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait generates YouTube thumbnails and saves every success to your gallery. Thumbnail Refine lets you adjust a near-winner with a short instruction instead of starting from scratch.

**The problem**
Creators regenerated entire batches to move text, brighten a background, or push an expression. They paid for four new images and often lost the composition they already liked. Most iterations are small edits, not blank-canvas jobs.

**The solution**
We built **Thumbnail Refine**. Open a saved thumbnail, describe one change in plain language, and get a new version derived from the original without restarting the whole form.

**How it works**
Hit Edit on a gallery card, enter a short instruction up to 500 characters, optionally add reference images, and submit. The server edits the existing image, stores a new thumbnail linked to the source, and charges credits only on success. Your original stays for side-by-side comparison. Same fairness rules as generation batches.

**The result**
Small fixes take one step. Edit sessions often follow generate sessions within minutes on the same thumbnail family. Iteration happens on near-winners instead of from a blank canvas every time.

**Bottom line**
Creative tools need a refine path, not just generate again. Preserve what works and charge fairly for the delta. See it at viewbait.app.
