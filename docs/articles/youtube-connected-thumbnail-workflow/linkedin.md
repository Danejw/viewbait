YouTube Connected Thumbnail Workflow

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait generates YouTube thumbnails with faces, styles, and palettes. On Pro, YouTube Studio Bridge connects your channel inside the studio so thumbnail work starts from real uploads, not a blank form.

**The problem**
Creators copied titles and notes from YouTube into ViewBait by hand. Context got lost between tabs. The thumbnail did not match what the video actually delivered, and publishing the winner meant another round of context switching.

**The solution**
We built **YouTube Studio Bridge**. Browse uploads, analyze the video, get 2 to 4 concept prompts grounded in the content, generate with that context, and optionally set the thumbnail on YouTube without leaving the app.

**How it works**
Connect on Pro. Open a video, run analysis, pick a concept. ViewBait fills the generator text and custom instructions from the video summary. Generate variations, pick a winner, and if you granted thumbnail permission, publish it back to the video. OAuth tokens and analysis stay server-side. If scope is missing, the UI tells you exactly how to reconnect.

**The result**
Thumbnail work starts from the upload list, not a blank studio. Pro users who connect YouTube generate more thumbnails per session. Concept-to-generate is the highest-intent path in the YouTube view.

**Bottom line**
When your product depends on external content, bring that content into the creation surface. Context should be one click, not a copy-paste chore. See it at viewbait.app.
