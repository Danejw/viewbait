Channel Consistency Check for Thumbnails

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait connects to YouTube on Pro so creators browse uploads and generate thumbnails in context. Channel Fit Check scores whether a new thumbnail still looks like the rest of your channel.

**The problem**
Creators feel when a thumbnail "does not look like us" but struggle to say why. A slightly different color grade here, a new font treatment there. By the time the browse page looks scattered, the damage is already published.

**The solution**
We built **Channel Fit Check**. Compare one video's thumbnail against up to ten others from your channel and get a 1 to 5 consistency score plus one or two plain cues like palette match or layout drift.

**How it works**
On the YouTube tab with your channel connected, tap consistency on any video card. ViewBait loads your thumbnail and sibling uploads from the grid, runs a vision comparison, and caches the result on the card. Low score means fix it before upload, not after analytics. Requires Pro with YouTube OAuth.

**The result**
Pro creators catch off-brand visuals during the upload workflow. Low scores on new uploads trigger a regenerate or style reset before publish instead of regret scrolling weeks later.

**Bottom line**
Relative scoring against the user's own library beats generic design advice. Drift is a comparison problem, not a taste problem. Try it at viewbait.app.
