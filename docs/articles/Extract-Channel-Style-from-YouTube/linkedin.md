Extract Channel Style from YouTube

**Project:** ViewBait.app
**Link:** https://viewbait.app

**The app**
ViewBait helps YouTube creators generate thumbnails with their face, colors, and saved styles. Channel Style Mirror learns that look from real uploads instead of asking you to invent it from memory.

**The problem**
One great thumbnail does not capture the pattern across your feed. Creators wrote style prompts from guesswork, then regenerated until something felt close. Colors shifted. Text treatment changed. Credits burned while the channel slowly stopped looking like itself.

**The solution**
We built **Channel Style Mirror**. Browse real uploads, pick 2 to 10 thumbnails that represent how you want to look, and ViewBait distills the shared color, layout, lighting, and mood into a reusable style.

**How it works**
Connect your channel on Pro, or paste any public channel URL on the Import tab. Select videos in the grid and hit Extract. The AI reads the set together, not one hero image. It saves a named style with reference images into your library. You rename or tweak the prompt once in the editor, then reuse it like any other preset. Saving custom styles requires Starter or above.

**The result**
New thumbnails start from your actual feed instead of a blank prompt. Creators extract once from five on-brand uploads and reuse that style on the next ten videos. Support questions shifted from "how do I match my channel?" to "which videos should I pick?"

**Bottom line**
When users need consistency, let them point at what already works and distill the pattern. Sample beats singleton every time. See it at viewbait.app.
